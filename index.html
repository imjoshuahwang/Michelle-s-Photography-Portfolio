About

Every day holds
something worth seeing.

I picked up a camera during a photography class and discovered something I didn't expect: the world looks different when you're paying attention. Not the grand moments — those photograph themselves.

It's the quiet ones I'm after. The afternoon light across a kitchen table. A child asleep in the back seat. Coffee going cold while someone reads. These are the photographs that actually last.

My work is unposed, unhurried, and honest. I photograph the people and places in my life the way I actually see them — full of texture, light, and imperfect humanity.

Approach

How I see the world

01

Unposed

No direction, no staging. I wait for the moment to arrive on its own terms. Authenticity can't be arranged — only noticed and not missed.

02

The grocery run, the afternoon light on a kitchen table, a kid asleep in the backseat. These are the photographs that actually last decades.
